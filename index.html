Let's Share

(Writing to my peers)


Machine learning is rapidly advancing, with more and more hypotheses, concepts, methods, experiments, datasets, and challenges emerging. For those of us who have been exposed to the academic field in the 20s, it's difficult to have a comprehensive understanding of the entire AI development, especially if we haven't experienced the deep learning wave. We often find ourselves trapped in a 'local optimum' situation within our specific domains, attempting to solve domain-specific problems solely with our domain knowledge, while neglecting the insights from developments in other fields. Hence, there is a need for us to gain an understanding of the progress in other domains under the broad theme of AI.

However, the AI landscape is vast and consists of numerous subfields, including NLP, CV, reliability, interpretability, and countless others. At the same time, we are aware of how challenging it can be to start learning from scratch in an unfamiliar field. So, why not help each other out?

For example, Zhang San might be well-versed in the development of NeRF in computer graphics, while Li Si might have a deep understanding of diffusion models. If each of us shares the excellent videos, articles, and insights we come across during our learning journeys, we can make it much easier to understand other domains. This transforms the process from being like a blind person touching an elephant to having a peer mentor guide us through, in the right sequence, to gain a proper understanding of the elephant in our minds.

Therefore, I'm sharing my contact information here, and I'll consider creating a group where we can all share good articles, insights, and tutorials.
