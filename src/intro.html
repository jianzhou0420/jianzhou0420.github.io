Jian Zhou is a second-year MPhil candidate at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), supervised by Prof. Qi Wu.

He has a solid background in the theoretical foundations of deep learning and Robotics, complemented by knowledge in control theory and robot kinematics, computer vision and 3D reconstruction, digital and analog circuits and electrical machinery, as well as mechanical structures and mechanics of materials.
He also has experiences on developing radar algorithms for tracking and vital sign detection.

His current research interests include imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and the design of vision-language-action (VLA) models, with the long-term goal of developing theoretically grounded methods for generalist household robots.

Prior to this, He has obtained:
MSc in Robotics and Computation at UCL
BEng in Mechatronics and Robotic Systems at the University of Liverpool and Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University
