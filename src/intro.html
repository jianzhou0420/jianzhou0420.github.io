Jian Zhou is a second-year MPhil candidate at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), supervised by Prof. Qi Wu.

He has a solid background in the theoretical foundations of deep learning and robotics, complemented by knowledge in control theory and robot kinematics, computer vision and 3D reconstruction, digital and analog circuits, electrical machinery, as well as mechanical structures and mechanics of materials. He also has experience developing radar algorithms for tracking and vital-sign detection.

His current research interests include imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and the design of vision-language-action (VLA) models, with the long-term goal of developing theoretically grounded methods for generalist household robots.
