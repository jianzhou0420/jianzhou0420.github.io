Jian Zhou is a second-year MPhil student at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), University of Adelaide, supervised by Prof. Qi Wu. His research lies in embodied AI, with a focus on embodied agentic systems for navigation, manipulation, and household robotics.

Before joining AIML, he completed his MSc at UCL, where he developed the computational side of his robotics background through computer vision, numerical optimization, 3D reconstruction, kinematics, and reinforcement learning. He received his BEng in Mechatronics and Robotic Systems from the University of Liverpool and Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University, where he built his foundation in robotic systems, electronics, electrical systems, and mechanical design.

His current research focuses on embodied agentic systems for household robots, exploring how future generalist embodied base models (VLA models, world-action models, or other emerging paradigms) can be orchestrated with perception, planning, and control modules to enable reliable physical interaction in everyday environments.
