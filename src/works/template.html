Decoupled Action Head: Confining Task Knowledge to Conditioning Layers

Jian Zhou, Sihao Lin, Shuai Fu, Qi Wu
Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), University of Adelaide
arXiv preprint, 2025
Paper (arXiv) Code (GitHub) Poster Slides

Abstract

[Your abstract goes here. Explain the problem you're solving, your approach, and the key results. For example:]

We introduce the Decoupled Action Head, a novel architecture that confines task-specific knowledge to conditioning layers while maintaining a shared representation space. Our approach addresses the challenge of multi-task learning in robotic manipulation by separating task-agnostic features from task-specific conditioning, enabling more efficient knowledge transfer and improved generalization across diverse manipulation tasks.

Key Contributions

  • Novel decoupled architecture that separates task knowledge into conditioning layers
  • Improved sample efficiency in multi-task imitation learning scenarios
  • State-of-the-art performance on benchmark robotic manipulation tasks
  • Extensive ablation studies demonstrating the effectiveness of the decoupling strategy

Method Overview

[Describe your method here. You can include diagrams, architecture figures, etc.]

Our approach consists of three main components: (1) a shared encoder that processes observations, (2) task-specific conditioning layers that modulate the representation based on task information, and (3) a shared action decoder that produces actions from the conditioned representations.

Results

We evaluate our method on several benchmark tasks and demonstrate significant improvements over baseline approaches.
