Why Embodied AI Will Converge on Agentic Systems

TL;DR. Whether you bet on atomic skills or on end-to-end VLA/WAM, embodied AI will enter the household as an agentic system.

The evolution of LLM agents suggests a pattern: once a foundation model becomes strong enough, its deployed form converges on the agentic paradigm — model + tools + an orchestration loop. We can expect household embodied robots to walk the same path once embodied foundation models mature. And compared to coding agents like Claude Code or Codex, embodied tasks will only depend more on the agentic system and its harness, not less. The reason starts with the combinatorial explosion of the task space.

A home is an open-world, long-horizon setting, and its complexity comes from two orthogonal dimensions: the composition across tasks, and the variation in detail within a single task. The first looks like "do the laundry, then clear the table, then open the packages" — the types and ordering of subtasks can be concatenated arbitrarily. The second shows up even if you look at "doing the laundry" alone: one household is meticulous — coats and underwear separated, hand-wash plus machine-wash; another household keeps it simple — three people's clothes tossed in together, trusting in "the power of chemistry." Multiply the permutations across tasks by the preferences within each task and the space is effectively infinite — you cannot train a separate end-to-end policy for every combination.

Step back, then, and suppose we don't chase "one policy per combination" but instead hope a single end-to-end foundation model will learn the shared structure on its own from massive data — absorbing the composition across tasks, the details within them, and the manipulation of all the objects each subtask touches. But measured against the data scales of language, multimodal, and video models, fitting embodied tasks by sheer data is likely to demand several more orders of magnitude — and across modalities far beyond text and image: proprioception, force/torque, depth, touch, and more. Collecting or simulating data at that scale, along with the compute, storage, and inference latency that follow, is extraordinarily hard. A telling sign: even in the LLM domain, where data is far more abundant than in embodied settings, the field did not end up purely end-to-end — it converged on agentic.

Step back once more, and suppose data is not the obstacle either: we genuinely obtain an embodied foundation model — a base model in the mold of GPT, Claude, or DeepSeek. It still has to be driven by human language: told what to do, whose preferences to honor, and to what standard. And reliably turning a natural-language instruction into a correctly completed task requires a harness layer to receive intent, orchestrate execution, and verify the result — exactly what Claude Code is to a coding model. The model is only the base; what turns it into a system that reliably gets the job done is the orchestration wrapped around it.

So we don't even need to invoke the stronger premise that "embodied tasks can be decomposed into atomic operations." Even standing entirely on the end-to-end position and retreating step by step — granting that a unified model is possible, granting that the data can be solved — we still arrive at the same place: for an embodied foundation model to actually land as a usable system, it must be wrapped in a layer of language-driven orchestration and verification, a harness. In other words, an agentic embodied system is not one route among several; it is the form the end-to-end ideal necessarily converges to once it meets the constraints of reality.
